# Kamikatsu

## Core idea

The Kamikatsu waste management categorizes inputs as the following: 
Paper (9 types): Includes newspapers/flyers, cardboard, magazines/mixed paper, paper cartons (rinsed/opened), paper cups, silver-lined cartons, stiff paper cores, shredded paper, and unrecyclable/dirty paper.
Plastics (6 types): PET bottles (caps/labels removed), white polystyrene trays, expanded polystyrene (Styrofoam), clean plastic bags, plastic caps, and other miscellaneous plastics.
Metals (5 types): Aluminum cans, steel cans, aerosol/spray cans, metal caps, and miscellaneous small metals.
Glass Bottles & Jars (4 types): Separated by color into clear, brown, other colors (green/blue), and reusable bottles (like 1-liter sake or beer bottles).
Dangerous Items (6 types): Ceramics/broken glass, mirrors/mercury thermometers, lightbulbs/fluorescent tubes, dry cell batteries, car batteries, and lighters.
Bulky Items (4 types): Bulky metal (bicycles), bulky wood, bulky fabric (carpets), and other non-recyclable bulky items like PVC or hoses.
Fabric & Clothing (2 types): Reusable clothing/blankets for the town’s thrift shop and unrecyclable/dirty fabrics.
Biomass (2 types): Wood/bamboo/cork products and used cooking oil (recycled into soap or fuel).
Incinerables (2 types): General items (rubber bands, footwear, masks) and biological waste (diapers, feminine hygiene products).
Other Specialized Categories: These include items residents must pay to dispose of (tires, fire extinguishers, large appliances) and a separate category for usable goods destined for the town's "Kuru-Kuru" reuse shop. 
Note that food waste is not collected at the center; residents are expected to compost it at home using town-subsidized electric composters or traditional methods.
