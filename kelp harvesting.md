# kelp harvesting

## Core idea
Harvesting seaweed to use for personal consumption does not require a license. To harvest more than 10 pounds you need a Commercial Kelp Harvester’s License. 

California Department of Fish and Wildlife (CDFW) rules:

Daily Bag Limit: You may harvest a maximum of 10 pounds wet weight per day in the aggregate of all marine algae.Prohibited Species: You are strictly forbidden from cutting, disturbing, or harvesting eelgrass, surfgrass, or sea palm.

Location Restrictions: Harvesting of any kind is entirely banned within State Marine Protected Areas (MPAs), Marine Reserves, and State Marine Parks.

Harvesting Method: You must harvest by hand or with simple hand tools. If collecting unattached, washed-up kelp from the sand, you can take the whole piece. If harvesting from live, attached kelp in the water, you cannot cut deeper than 4 feet below the water's surface.

You will need: 
  - cutting tool
  - neoprene gloves
  - high traction shoes
  - a mesh bag or wire basket
  - tide chart
  - marine map
  - fishing license?
  - warm, water resistant clothes
  - whistle
  - insulated cooler
  - digital scale

Timing:
Early morning during a "minus tide" (a low tide below 0.0 feet), arriving at the beach 1 to 2 hours before the lowest point.
April - July
New moon or Full moon
