# xenia

## Core idea
In ancient Greece, xenia (pronounced ZEE-nee-uh) is the sacred, institutionalized concept of hospitality or "guest-friendship". It is rooted in generosity, gift exchange, and a reciprocal social contract between a host and a stranger.The Rules: Hosts were expected to provide food, shelter, and a bath to travelers before asking their names. Guests were expected to be courteous and not overstay their welcome.Divine Protection: Because travelers could be gods in disguise, xenia was considered a moral obligation protected by Zeus, known as Zeus Xenios (the patron of guests and strangers). Breaking these rules was believed to invite divine wrath.
